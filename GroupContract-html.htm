ARTICLE I: LEADERSHIP POLICY AND OTHER MEMBER ROLES

A. Leadership Selection

a. The team leader for each assignment will rotate alphabetically according to first name starting with Chris Govas for assignment #1.

B. Leadership Roles

a. The team leader is responsible for:

i. The final editing process of each assignment.

ii. Facilitating the democratic delegation of tasks to each group member.

iii. Leading each group meeting adhering to the meeting process detailed in Article III.

iv. The submission of the final assignment.

C. Scribe Roles (Hridi Das)

a. The scribe is responsible for:

i. Keeping detailed minutes of each meeting

ii. Reading the minutes of the previous meeting at the start of each meeting

ARTICLE II: MEETING LOGISTICS

A. Location

a. The location of the meetings will be held online using the application Google Hangouts.

B. Date/Time

a. The first meeting will be held on Tuesday Sept 15th at 2:00pm.

b. The day of each meeting will be Tuesday whereas the time of each subsequent meeting will be found out using an online scheduling service called Doodle.

c. If day/time conflicts arise later in the semester, a new meeting date will be selected using doodle.

C. Duration

a. The duration of each meeting should be around 30 minutes or until all relevant details have been thoroughly discussed.

ARTICLE III: MEETING STRUCTURE

A. The meeting structure is as follows:

a. Meetings will begin within 5 minutes of the scheduled time or when everyone is present

b. The minutes from the previous meeting will be read by the Scribe.

c. The team leader will present any relevant information regarding performance on the previous assignment

i. If any performance issues were brought up, a team wide discussion will take place on how to avoid these issues on future assignments

d. The new assignment will be discussed

e. The assignment will be split up evenly based on difficulty and each task will be assigned to a team member to complete 24 hours before the due date.

f. Discussion of the time of the next meeting

g. End of meeting

ARTICLE IV: TARDINESS, ABSENTEEISM, POOR PERFORMANCE, CONFLICTS

A. Late/Absent Policy

a. If a group member is late to a meeting, a text message will be sent to his/her phone reminding them of the meeting.

b. If the group member is absent for the duration of the meeting, the group leader will send an e-mail to them and that group member will have to explain themselves at the next meeting.

c. If a group member or leader is late more than three times at group meetings or is absent more than two times, the professor will be notified.

d. If the group member has a very good reason for not being at a meeting, that meeting absence will not count towards the values mentioned in IV b.c.

B. Poor Performance

a. If more than two members in the group have concerns about the poor performance on an assignment by another group member, that group member will be notified immediately and the group will guide that member in hopes of improving the assignment.

b. If the same group member consistently disregards the advice of his peers and continues producing poor quality work, the professor will be notified of his/her poor performance.

c. If a group member cannot be contacted for their contribution to the assignment and said assignment is due in 24 hours, the other members of the group will complete that portion of the assignment for the missing member.

d. If a group member is chronically rude or unproductive in group meetings, the professor will be notified.

C. Conflicts

a. All conflicts should be handled with the integrity that is expected of students attending a university in Canada.

b. If a conflict should arise between two group members regarding the unfair delegation of tasks in an assignment, the entire group will split the task in a different way that is agreeable to everyone.

c. Should personal conflicts arise between two group members, those two members are responsible for making amends and/or not letting these conflicts affect group meetings and productivity.

d. If the group does not know how to effectively deal with a certain conflict that has arisen in the group for whatever reason, the advice of the TA/GAs or the professor will be sought out in order to move forward.

ARTICLE V: GRADES

A. Quality

a. The grade that the group will be given is indicative of the amount of time and effort put into the assignment. It is up to each group member to put forth their best work. Consequences of poor quality work bring the rest of the team down, and will be dealt with in accordance to Article IV
